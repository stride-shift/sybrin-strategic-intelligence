# Jobs-To-Be-Done Analysis: Top 4 Markets (Data-Grounded)

**Markets**: Kenya, Zambia, Tanzania, Philippines
**Methodology**: Clayton Christensen JTBD framework applied to documented customer challenges
**Data Source**: 248 research documents with 140+ named financial institutions
**Focus**: Jobs derived from actual customer pain points, then white space identification

**Date**: November 16, 2025

---

## Methodology Difference from Previous Analysis

**Previous approach**: Applied JTBD framework based on expert knowledge and market assumptions.

**This approach**:
1. Start with **actual documented customer challenges** from 140+ named institutions
2. Translate those challenges into JTBD statements
3. Identify **gaps** between what customers are trying to do and available solutions
4. Find white space opportunities

---

## Executive Summary: What the Data Shows

### Customer Intelligence Coverage

- **Kenya**: 40+ named institutions with specific documented challenges
- **Zambia**: 30+ named institutions (heavy MFI/rural focus)
- **Tanzania**: 25+ named institutions (post-core-migration opportunities)
- **Philippines**: 50+ named institutions (most comprehensive data)

### Top Jobs Emerging from Customer Data

**Most urgent jobs** (documented across multiple institutions):
1. **Comply with new real-time fraud mandates** (Philippines AFASA, Kenya check decommissioning)
2. **Scale digital channels without operational meltdown** (Equity Bank 98% target, NMB 6M users)
3. **Serve rural/unbanked profitably** (CARD Bank 329 MBOs, Agora 90% rural clients)
4. **Integrate with instant payment systems** (TIPS Tanzania, iPSO Kenya, InstaPay Philippines)
5. **Digitize agent networks at scale** (Faulu 600+ agents, Zanaco 32k agents, KWFT 231 branches)

### Key Finding: Emotional/Social Jobs Are Invisible in Research

The research documents **functional** challenges extensively but **rarely mentions**:
- How decision-makers want to feel
- Social/reputational concerns
- Fear of obsolescence or competitive displacement

This suggests either:
- Customers don't articulate these needs (but still hire/fire solutions based on them)
- Research didn't probe for emotional/social jobs
- **White space opportunity**: Solutions that address unstated emotional/social jobs

## Part 1: Jobs Derived from Documented Customer Challenges

### Job Category A: Regulatory Compliance Jobs

#### Job 1: Meet BSP AFASA real-time fraud mandate by 2025 without breaking IT budget

**Documented evidence**:
- Philippines: "AFASA/real-time fraud detection compliance by 2025; urgent need for affordable fraud management" (thrift/rural banks segment)
- "BAP cybersecurity initiative; AFASA real-time fraud mandate" (44 member banks)
- CARD Bank, PSBank, AllBank, rural banks segment: "Legacy or EOL core banking systems...limited IT budgets...AFASA compliance pressure"

**Job statement**: Achieve full BSP AFASA compliance for real-time fraud detection when IT budgets are constrained and core systems are legacy/EOL.

**Circumstances**:
- 2025 compliance deadline approaching
- Thrift/rural banks with PHP 50-200M budgets
- Legacy cores can't support real-time monitoring
- No in-house fraud expertise

**Current solutions hired**:
- Emergency vendor RFPs
- Hoping for BSP forbearance/extensions
- Consortium deals through BAP
- **Sybrin's Tizana platform** (positioned as BAP-endorsed, multi-tenant SaaS for $500k-2M consortium deal)

**Success criteria**:
- Clean BSP examinations
- Real-time transaction monitoring operational
- Cost <5% of IT budget
- No major fraud losses during implementation

**UNDERSERVED**:
- Most enterprise fraud platforms (FICO, SAS) price out thrift/rural banks
- Compliance is binary (pass/fail) but solutions are priced on continuous value scale
- **Gap**: Compliance-as-a-service that guarantees regulatory pass for fixed cost

---

#### Job 2: Navigate check decommissioning without losing revenue or customers

**Documented evidence**:
- Kenya: "Active regulatory drivers (instant payments, check decommissioning)" (geography ranking)
- Zambia: "E-check opportunity as checks decommission" (Zambia #2 ranking)
- Kenya segment: "Check decommissioning (Zambia/Malawi/Kenya)" (emotional job: feel in control when regulators impose sudden requirements)

**Job statement**: Replace check-based revenue streams and customer workflows when regulators mandate check decommissioning with short timelines.

**Circumstances**:
- Central bank announces check phase-out (12-24 month timeline)
- Corporate customers still want paper-based controls
- Float income from check clearing disappears
- Staff trained on check processing, not digital payments

**Current solutions hired**:
- InstaPay/PesaLink integration (Kenya)
- E-check platforms (Zambia opportunity)
- Manual workarounds (printable digital receipts)
- Customer education campaigns

**Success criteria**:
- Zero customer attrition
- Replacement revenue from digital transaction fees
- Compliance with decommissioning timeline
- Staff successfully retrained

**UNDERSERVED**:
- Most instant payment solutions don't replicate check workflow benefits (audit trail, stop-payment, reconciliation)
- Revenue replacement strategies are ad-hoc
- **Gap**: "Digital check" products that preserve workflow/control benefits of paper checks but use instant payment rails

### Job Category B: Scaling Digital Operations Jobs

#### Job 3: Reach 98% self-service transactions without losing the 20% of customers who can't/won't go digital

**Documented evidence**:
- Kenya: "Equity Bank targeting 98% of transactions via self-service channels; already at 86% digital" (documented need)
- Kenya: "Co-operative Bank 92% digital transaction share" (biometric bank vision, branch digitization)
- Kenya: "Absa Kenya 94% digital" (high digital transaction share)

**Job statement**: Push digital adoption from 86-94% to 98%+ while retaining elderly, rural, and digitally illiterate customers who prefer branches/agents.

**Circumstances**:
- Cost-to-serve via branch is 10x digital
- Board pressures for cost reduction
- Competitors at 95%+ digital
- But 2-15% of customer base (often high-value or inclusion mandate) can't/won't adopt

**Current solutions hired**:
- Forced migration (close branches, charge for teller transactions)
- Assisted digital (staff help customers use app in branch)
- Agent networks as bridge
- "Digital champions" among customers

**Success criteria**:
- 98%+ digital transaction share
- Customer NPS maintained or improved
- No regulatory penalties for financial exclusion
- Cost-to-serve reduced 40%+

**UNDERSERVED**:
- Solutions focus on making digital easier (UX) or forcing migration (fees), not on understanding WHY customers resist
- **Gap**: Transition management tools that identify resistance reasons per customer and provide targeted intervention (literacy, trust, access, preference)

---

#### Job 4: Manage 6M+ digital users with same operational capacity as 1M users

**Documented evidence**:
- Tanzania: "NMB Bank large and fast-growing digital user base (6M+ digital users) drives need for advanced fraud management, AI/ML risk management, and digital onboarding modules"
- Tanzania: "Uses Oracle Flexcube; potential need for integrated document management and workflow enhancements"

**Job statement**: Scale digital user base 5-10x without proportional increase in fraud losses, customer service staff, or operational costs.

**Circumstances**:
- Digital acquisition costs are low (vs. branch)
- But fraud rates, chargebacks, and support queries scale with volume
- Legacy manual review processes can't keep up
- Board expects margin expansion from digitalization, not just revenue growth

**Current solutions hired**:
- AI/ML fraud models (mentioned as NMB need)
- Automated KYC/onboarding
- Chatbots for customer service
- Workflow automation tools

**Success criteria**:
- 5-10x user growth
- Fraud rate maintained or improved (as % of transactions)
- Customer service FTE growth <20% despite user growth
- Operational cost per user decreases 50%+

**UNDERSERVED**:
- AI/ML fraud models exist but require expensive data science teams to maintain
- **Gap**: "Self-learning" fraud platforms that auto-tune models based on local patterns without data scientists

### Job Category C: Rural/Inclusion Jobs

#### Job 5: Serve 90% rural clients profitably when connectivity is poor and field officers travel 100km on bikes

**Documented evidence**:
- Zambia: "Agora Microfinance Zambia (AMZ) - Presence in all 10 provinces with 90%+ rural clients, poor connectivity and bike-based field officers; needs offline-capable ID verification, mobile biometrics, rural-optimized solutions"
- Zambia MFI segment: "Poor network coverage in rural areas, heavy dependence on agents and field officers, requiring offline-capable and mobile-first solutions"

**Job statement**: Originate loans, verify identity, and collect repayments in rural areas when staff travel by bike, mobile networks are unreliable, and customers lack formal IDs.

**Circumstances**:
- Target market is unbanked rural poor (financial inclusion mandate)
- Field officers on motorcycles/bikes visiting villages
- 2G coverage or no coverage
- Customers have no national ID, proof of address, or formal employment

**Current solutions hired**:
- Paper forms (officers carry back to office for manual entry)
- Community vouching/group guarantee for identity
- Cash repayment collection at village meetings
- "Know your customer" through field officer relationships

**Success criteria**:
- Loan turnaround time <7 days
- ID verification fraud <1%
- Field officer productivity (clients/day) 2x
- Offline capability for 100% of workflows

**UNDERSERVED**:
- Most digital onboarding requires online connectivity
- Biometric devices don't work offline or are too expensive for MFI budgets
- **Gap**: Offline-first biometric + alternative ID verification (social vouching, behavioral data, phone metadata) that syncs when connectivity available

#### Job 6: Manage 329 Micro Banking Offices with same oversight as 54 branches

**Documented evidence**:
- Philippines: "CARD Bank - 900K+ clients, 54 branches, 329 Micro Banking Offices; strong financial inclusion mandate. Needs enhanced mobile banking (konek2CARD), agent banking for MBOs, digital credit scoring, financial inclusion platforms, low-cost onboarding, and low-bandwidth solutions"

**Job statement**: Supervise and support 329 distributed micro-offices with tiny transaction volumes when branch management systems assume 20-50 large branches.

**Circumstances**:
- Average MBO has <3 staff, <500 customers
- MBOs are in remote barrios (villages) with poor connectivity
- Centralized compliance/risk systems can't see MBO-level detail
- Local staff lack banking expertise (often community volunteers)

**Current solutions hired**:
- Manual oversight (area managers visit monthly)
- Agent banking platforms adapted for MBOs
- konek2CARD mobile banking (CARD Bank specific)
- SMS-based transaction reporting

**Success criteria**:
- Real-time visibility into all 329 MBO transactions
- Fraud/error detection at MBO level
- Compliance reporting consolidated across MBOs
- MBO staff can operate with <1 day training

**UNDERSERVED**:
- Branch management systems don't scale to 300+ micro-sites
- Agent banking platforms assume higher transaction volumes
- **Gap**: Micro-branch network management platform (multi-tenant SaaS for 100-500 sites, offline-capable, simplified compliance)

### Job Category D: Payment Integration Jobs

#### Job 7: Connect to TIPS without replacing our core banking system

**Documented evidence**:
- Tanzania: "TIPS (Tanzania Instant Payment System) - 45+ institutions connected; 490M+ transactions in 2024, creating capacity, reliability, and integration-engine needs for all connected banks/MFIs"
- Tanzania segment: "Banks (CRDB, NMB, NBC...) common needs: TIPS integration, fraud and risk management (especially digital/mobile), digital onboarding and KYC/AML automation"

**Job statement**: Meet central bank mandate to connect to instant payment system when our core banking system lacks modern APIs and integration will cost 12+ months and $500k+.

**Circumstances**:
- Central bank mandates TIPS/instant payment connection (compliance deadline)
- Legacy core lacks ISO 20022 support
- Core vendor quotes $500k-1M and 18-month timeline
- Can't afford/risk full core replacement

**Current solutions hired**:
- Payment gateway/middleware (sits between core and TIPS)
- Expensive core vendor "integration module"
- Manual workarounds (batch file transfers)

**Success criteria**:
- TIPS connection operational before deadline
- Real-time settlement (not batch)
- Cost <$200k
- Timeline <6 months
- No core replacement required

**UNDERSERVED**:
- Payment gateways exist but most require deep core integration anyway
- **Gap**: "TIPS-as-a-Service" - fully managed instant payment connectivity that abstracts core system complexity (core-agnostic facade)

#### Job 8: Process InstaPay/PESONet at scale without building expensive real-time infrastructure

**Documented evidence**:
- Philippines segment: "Intense competition from digital banks and e-wallets (GCash, Maya, etc.), especially around 5-minute digital onboarding and 24/7 services"
- Philippines banks: "Manual/paper-based KYC/AML; need for digital onboarding and compliance case management"
- Philippines: "InstaPay/PESONet integration difficulty" (thrift/rural bank challenges)

**Job statement**: Offer 24/7 instant payments (InstaPay) and batch settlements (PESONet) when we lack real-time core systems and 24/7 operations staff.

**Circumstances**:
- Customers expect instant transfers (GCash/Maya set standard)
- Our core runs batch processing overnight
- Weekend/holiday operations require expensive staffing
- Can't compete on speed with digital banks

**Current solutions hired**:
- Third-party processors (e.g., Misys/BancNet for PESONet)
- Manual workarounds (queuing transactions for Monday processing)
- Partner with digital banks (white-label their instant payment capability)

**Success criteria**:
- 24/7/365 instant payment availability
- Processing cost <PHP 5 per transaction
- No core system replacement
- Compliance with BSP instant payment rules

**UNDERSERVED**:
- Most processors still require significant core integration
- **Gap**: Instant payment facade that works with ANY core (even batch-mode cores) by maintaining transaction state externally and syncing to core when available

## Part 2: Emotional and Social Jobs (Inferred from Context)

The research documents functional jobs extensively but **never directly captures emotional or social jobs**. However, we can infer these from the documented situations and universal human motivations.

### Emotional Jobs for Executives (CEO/MD Level)

#### Emotional Job E1: Sleep at night knowing we won't fail the regulatory exam

**Context inference**:
- Philippines AFASA 2025 deadline with penalties for non-compliance
- Multiple mentions of "regulatory pressure," "BSP examinations," "compliance deadlines"
- Rural banks with limited IT budgets facing same rules as Tier 1 banks

**Job statement**: Eliminate the 2am anxiety about "what if we fail the BSP exam and I'm personally liable?"

**Why unstated**:
- Executives don't admit fear in research interviews
- "Regulatory compliance" sounds professional; "I'm scared of being fired" doesn't

**Current solutions hired**:
- Hiring expensive consultants (for blame-shifting if it fails)
- Over-spending on compliance (buying peace of mind)
- Delay/avoidance (hope problem goes away)

**Success criteria**:
- Can confidently tell board "we will pass"
- Sleep improves 3 months before exam
- No personal liability exposure
- Have someone to blame if it goes wrong

**White space opportunity**: "Compliance guarantee with executive liability protection" - vendor contractually accepts liability for exam failure, including executive legal defense costs.

#### Emotional Job E2: Avoid being the CEO who killed the bank with a bad technology decision

**Context inference**:
- Tanzania: "Legacy or EOL core banking systems"
- Philippines: Multiple mentions of "core banking on Temenos/Flexcube" (multi-million $ bets)
- Investrust Bank Zambia closure (2024) = cautionary tale

**Why unstated**:

## Part 3: Jobs MISSING from Research (White Space Identification)

The research extensively documents **functional operational challenges** but is silent on several job categories that JTBD theory suggests exist:

### Category A: Strategic Decision Jobs (Not Documented)

#### Undocumented Job: Choose technology investments that won't become obsolete before ROI

**Why it should exist**:
- Multiple institutions mention "core banking on Temenos" or "migration to Flexcube" = multi-year, multi-million $ bets
- Technology cycles are shortening (cloud, AI, open banking)
- Decision-makers have been burned before (mentions of "legacy/EOL systems")

**Why not documented**:
- Research focused on operational pain points, not executive decision anxiety
- Customers don't articulate this as "job to be done" (it's emotional/social)

**Evidence it exists (indirect)**:
- Tanzania: "Azania Bank recently launched new digital platform (Feb 2025), creating opportunity for add-on modules rather than a new core" = fear of core lock-in, preference for modular
- Philippines: "Legacy or EOL core banking systems" = consequence of bad past decisions

**Job statement**: Make technology investment decisions that protect against obsolescence when vendor roadmaps are unreliable and our own 5-year strategy is uncertain.

**Unmet needs**:
- Investment protection clauses (if vendor pivots, we get refund/transition)
- Consumption-based pricing (pay for value delivered, not upfront licenses)
- Modularity that allows mid-stream pivots
- Exit guarantees (data portability, no lock-in)

**White space**: "Hedge" technology procurement models that share obsolescence risk between vendor and customer

---

#### Undocumented Job: Demonstrate innovation to board without betting the bank

**Why it should exist**:
- CRDB, NMB, Equity Bank, KCB are all described as "digital leaders" = competitive positioning
- Board/shareholders demand innovation
- But failed pilots damage reputation

**Why not documented**:
- This is social job (how CEO/CIO wants to be perceived)
- Research asked "what do you need?" not "what do you fear?"

**Job statement**: Show board and market that we're innovative leaders while avoiding failed pilots that damage my credibility and the bank's reputation.

**Unmet needs**:
- Innovation validation frameworks (how to de-risk pilots)
- Pilot-to-production conversion guarantees
- Innovation ROI dashboards that board understands
- "Safe" innovation (proven-elsewhere, not bleeding-edge)

## Part 4: High-Priority White Space (Research Gaps + Underserved Jobs)

### White Space #1: Compliance-as-a-Regulatory-Pass (Not Just Tools)

**Job**: Pass regulatory examinations with zero findings while spending <5% of IT budget.

**Current market**: Compliance tools (KYC, AML, fraud platforms) that help you comply but don't guarantee regulatory pass.

**Gap**: No vendor shares regulatory risk. If you buy FICO fraud platform and still fail BSP examination, that's your problem.

**Opportunity**: "Compliance-as-a-Service" with regulatory pass guarantee:
- Fixed price for regulatory outcome (not tool license)
- If customer fails exam with our platform in place, we cover remediation + penalties
- Revenue model: success fee for clean exams, not upfront licenses

**Addressable customers**:
- Philippines 400+ rural/thrift banks facing AFASA deadline
- Kenya Tier 3 banks (8 named)
- Zambia MFIs/small banks

---

### White Space #2: Instant Payments for Batch-Mode Cores

**Job**: Offer 24/7 instant payments when core banking system only runs batch processing.

**Current market**: Payment gateways that still require core integration, or expensive core upgrades.

**Gap**: No solution for "instant payment facade" that works with truly legacy batch-only cores.

**Opportunity**: External transaction state management:
- Instant payment transactions processed and settled in real-time by facade
- Facade maintains authoritative transaction ledger
- Syncs to batch core overnight
- Core sees it as normal batch file, but customers experience real-time

**Addressable customers**:
- Tanzania community banks (Mucoba, Maendeleo, Uchumi)
- Philippines rural banks with legacy cores
- Any institution with EOL core that can't afford replacement

---

### White Space #3: MFI Micro-Branch Network Management

**Job**: Manage 300+ micro-branches/offices with same oversight as 50 branches.

**Current market**: Branch management for 10-100 locations, or agent banking (but MBOs aren't agents).

**Gap**: Nothing purpose-built for 200-500 micro-sites with <3 staff each.

**Opportunity**: Micro-branch SaaS platform:
- Multi-tenant (affordable for MFIs)
- Offline-first (rural connectivity)
- Simplified compliance (MBO staff aren't bankers)
- Network-level analytics (detect problems across 300 sites)

**Addressable customers**:
- CARD Bank (329 MBOs) = reference customer
- Kenya MFIs with branch + agent hybrid (Faulu, KWFT)
- Any financial inclusion institution with >100 access points

---

### White Space #4: Profitability Transition Management (Not Just Digital Migration)

**Job**: Move customers from 86% digital to 98% digital without losing the 12% who can't transition.

**Current market**: Digital banking platforms (focus on UX), forced migration (fees/closures).

**Gap**: No tools for diagnosing WHY specific customers resist and providing targeted intervention.

**Opportunity**: Customer transition intelligence platform:
- Analyzes transaction patterns to identify resistance reasons (literacy vs. trust vs. access vs. preference)
- Prescribes interventions per customer (training, assisted digital, agent bridge, exception handling)
- Measures transition success and adjusts

**Addressable customers**:
- Equity Bank (targeting 98% from 86%)
- Co-op Bank, Absa Kenya (already 92-94%, want higher)
- Any bank with board pressure for digital % but financial inclusion mandate

---

### White Space #5: Agent Fraud Prevention (Not Just Customer Fraud)

**Job**: Prevent agent fraud without expensive monitoring staff or devices.

**Current market**: Fraud platforms focus on customer-to-bank fraud, not agent-to-customer fraud.

**Gap**: Agent-side fraud is huge risk but underserved (agents stealing from customers, fake deposits, identity theft).

**Opportunity**: Peer agent comparison + behavioral fraud detection:
- Compare agent patterns (if one agent's customers have 10x charge-backs, flag)
- Offline anomaly detection (device-based, syncs when connected)
- Ultra-low-cost (feature-phone compatible, USSD fraud alerts)

**Addressable customers**:
- Zanaco (32k agents)
- Faulu (600+ agents)
- Mobile money operators (Airtel Money, MTN MoMo)
