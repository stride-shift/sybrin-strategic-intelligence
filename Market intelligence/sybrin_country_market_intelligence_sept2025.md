Commodity-dependent economies (Zambia, Ghana, Botswana) show higher volatility requiring flexible commercial models. Transaction-based pricing aligns with economic cycles.

Diversified economies (Kenya, South Africa) provide stable demand but intense competition. Differentiation through specialized solutions becomes critical.

Small economies (Rwanda, Lesotho) offer limited scale but strategic value through regional integration roles. These markets provide testing grounds for innovative solutions.

### Optimal Resource Allocation

Concentrate resources in markets with:
- Regulatory mandates (Zambia cheque discontinuation)
- Government support (Rwanda fintech strategy, Ghana SME fund)
- Existing dominance (Kenya infrastructure, South Africa relationships)
- Greenfield opportunities (Philippines digital banks)

Avoid overinvestment in:
- Mature markets without clear catalysts
- Countries with established dominant competitors
- Markets requiring significant localization without commensurate returns

The 18-month timeline demands focus on quick wins rather than long-term market development. Prioritize markets with existing relationships, regulatory tailwinds, and clear revenue visibility.
